Mbti Cognitive Functions

Si: Making connections of the current situation with past experiences. IT IS NOT THE FUNCTION OF HAVING A GOOD MEMORY. MEMORY IS NOT A COGNITIVE FUNCTION IN MBTI. They are willing to share and bring out their past experiences during conversations. People who have this function tend to talk about past experiences with others while comparing and contrasting. Doing things by the book. Rarely improvising tasks. Not always questioning authority.

Se: All about living in the moment while being clear from any thoughts. Taking risks without considering the consequences, playing sports for fun, and doing things just for the sake of it is considered Se.

Fi: Values. Doing stuff that one thinks to be right without the heavy consideration of Logic. Fi is linked with Morality. If one feels like they should not do a certain action because they feel like it is wrong in a moral sense. They will often not do such a thing. Empathy if they relate to the subject.

Fe: Maintaining a harmony vibe with everyone around them. Fe is most associated with Manipulation since others think they are putting on a nice act in order to get close with others. Many times this is not the case. In society, there are always nice people and bad people. Anyone can fall in those categories. Empathy without relating.

Ti: Easiest way to describe logic. They want to understand everything in the realm of information and be consistently accurate. This does not mean people who do not have this function, do not want or use logic. The people who have Ti, want to navigate life with the most logical path. Often setting aside F

Te: Has some logical sense. Instead of logic driving the path for them, they focus on all the information and tools that can lead them to success. Also includes setting aside Feelings since they sometimes get in the way of production. Te may become a nerd, but only on specific subjects that they deem is useful.

Ni: NOT THE STANDARD INTUITION. SOMEONE WHO HAS NI DOES NOT HAVE THAT SUPERNATURAL POWER. Often confused with their sensing counterpart, Si. What this function does differently is that they think about the future more. What the current situation may lead to. Narrowing. Focused on how events will affect them

Ne: Considering potential possibilities that may happen. Even if it has a very low chance of happening. Providing multiple ways to do a certain task. NOT SOMEONE WHO IS RANDOM AND TROLLS ALOT. Everyone who is random and trolls alot does not 100% have NE. Focused on the outside world.

Mbti Function Stack

1: Your strongest function. The one you are constantly displaying (Dominant)

2: Your second function that is the opposite I-E of your first function while being the T/F or N/S that you did not use. This is also the function that prevents making your inferior looking too weak (Parent)

3: Opposite of your second function. Secondary option for your first to work with. Sometimes in a bad way called a “loop”. The loop 100% ignores the second function (Child)

4: Your weakest. Insecure. Opposite of the first function. The aspect in life you want to control. In order to engage in this activity, you must turn off your first function. (Inferior)

7: The I/E of your third function. The actual “weakest” of your functions (Blind)

Enneagram

Heart

Type 2: These People tend to care about others. They take on the impression of being generous and helpful.

Type 3: Trying to be the best at a subject, sometimes even everything. They set their goals to be on the top. Success Oriented

Type 4: Most prone to emotions out of all Enneagram types. This person wants to forge their own path. Does not have to have to be an achiever.

Head

Type 5: Often described as a resourceful person. Hoards stuff in an efficient manner. Having the need to gather as much information as possible in a certain manner.

Type 6: Responsible person who is loyal to their own group. Maximizes Security. Most likely to be the most anxious

Type 7: Their goal is to have a fun and enjoyable experience. Often linked with Spontaneous and Versatile

Gut

Type 8: They seek power to shape things into their way. Often confident.

Type 9: Easygoing. Very agreeable. Being comfortable. Not an immediate threat. Will go out of their way to maintain the peace around them.

Type 1: Responsible person who tries to do their job as accurately as they can (Perfectionist). Often following their own system of principles (which sometimes may be influenced by an authority)


Tritype

A person will not fall under one personality of the enneagram. Tritype is a form of a subtype theory on top of the enneagram. To find your tritype, you put one number from the Heart, Head and Gut, into a 3 digit number. The first number should be your core, the second number is the type out of one of the three in the HHG that you haven't used while representing you the most. The third and last number for your tritype is from the last group that you have not used yet (and represents you the most out of the 3). 269, 358, 164 are existing theories. However 561, 123, and 987 are not.

Wings

For each enneagram in your tritype (core included). There is something called a wing. The wing is the form of a “w” that connects one number with another number. The other number is either lower or a higher value with a difference of 1. For example 5w6 is an existing subtype. You can also do 5w4. However you cannot do 5w7 5w3, etc. There are only two subtypes for each core. If you are a 9 or a 1. You are allowed to be “9w1” or “1w9”. The wing should be the side you act the closest to from your core.

Socionics

Leading: What you do actively and passively. Prevents the ignoring function from being active

Creative: Sharing it with others. What you are comfortable talking about.

Role: Focuses on how this function can affect the leading (S/N or T/F of your First Function)

Vulnerable: What you do not look out for (S/N or T/F of your second function)

Suggestive: What the user is overwhelmed by. Limits how much the user can use this function in a period of time. (Opposite of your first)

Mobilizing: What you are motivated by. (Opposite of your second)

Ignoring: Exists to tell the user what not to do (actively). Not the function that the user does not want. (I/E of your first)

Demonstrative: 2nd Strongest. You only actively use it if you have to. (I/E of your second)

Aspects

First position: The one you are confident in doing. Your strongest

Second position: The one you are comfortable sharing it with the outside world while maintaining the confidence as the first block

Third position: Depends on the one what is in the first two spots to navigate the world. It's something you don't want to do but have to based on your morals and nature. (Similar to how Inferior function works in Mbti)

Fourth position: You are willing to engage in this topic, but you don't really care what happens to it.

Detailed Third Position

If you have L in your third position and Emotion in one of the first two you have a tendency to act stupid. Whether on purpose or not

If you have E in your third Emotion aspect for your third position and logic in one of the first two. You have the tendency to act and suppress your emotions

If you have F in your third position and V in the first two, you have a tendency to be resourceful

If you have V in your third position and F in one of the first two, you have a tendency to have a strong sense of duty

Other Specifics

If you have both F and V as your first two spots,you do not care how you get your work done. Just do it and get it over.

If you have both L and E as your first two spots, you have a strong correlation with identity.
